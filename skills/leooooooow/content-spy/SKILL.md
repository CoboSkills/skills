# Content Spy

Understanding what your competitors post — and when — is one of the highest-leverage activities an ecommerce operator can do. Content Spy transforms raw competitor observation into structured competitive intelligence: documenting content cadence, identifying recurring themes, decoding hook formulas, and mapping promotional timing so you can program against them rather than alongside them.

## Use when

- You want to reverse-engineer a competitor TikTok Shop account's posting schedule and understand which days and times they push promotional content versus organic educational content, especially before a major sale event like 11.11 or Black Friday.
- A product category you sell in is suddenly surging in competitor content volume and you need to understand whether it is a paid push, a trend response, or a platform algorithm play before deciding whether to match or counter-program.
- You are launching a new product and want to audit the top 5 organic creators in your niche to identify hook structures, video lengths, and CTA styles that are generating consistent views and purchase conversions.
- You have noticed a competitor's affiliate or KOL network expanding and want to map which creators they are activating, at what frequency, and what content formats are being used across that network over the past 30 days.

## What this skill does

Content Spy takes a structured list of competitor accounts, content samples, or post descriptions and produces a detailed competitive content audit. It categorizes each piece of content by format type — tutorial, unboxing, testimonial, price promotion, or live replay — identifies recurring hook patterns and opening-frame strategies, maps posting frequency by day and hour where data is provided, and highlights promotional clusters tied to sale events or new product launches. The output surfaces specific gaps in competitor programming where your content can dominate with less noise, and counter-scheduling opportunities where posting at certain times means your content surfaces without direct head-to-head competition from the brands you are tracking.

## Inputs required

- **competitor_accounts** (required): A list of 1–10 competitor TikTok Shop, Instagram, or YouTube handles or page URLs you want to analyze — for example "@brandX", "@shopY", or a product category search result page showing top performers.
- **content_samples** (required): 10–50 post titles, captions, descriptions, video transcripts, or structured notes about what you observed in those accounts. The more samples across different dates, the richer the pattern analysis will be.
- **your_category** (required): Your product category and key selling proposition — for example "premium Korean skincare for women aged 25–35" — so the gap analysis is framed specifically against your positioning rather than the category as a whole.
- **analysis_window** (optional): The time period covered by the content samples, such as "last 30 days" or "Q4 2025", to calibrate frequency estimates and identify whether patterns are seasonal or structural.

## Output format

The output is a structured competitive content intelligence report divided into four clearly labeled parts. Part one is a Posting Frequency Map showing estimated post volume by day of week and format type in table format. Part two is a Hook and Theme Taxonomy listing the top five to eight recurring hook formulas found in competitor content — for example "This dollar-X product did Y" or "Stop doing Z if you want W" — with example openings and an estimated engagement tier for each. Part three is a Promotional Calendar Reconstruction showing identified sale pushes, product launch windows, and affiliate activation clusters with approximate timing. Part four is a Gap and Counter-Programming Opportunities section with three to five specific content angles that are underserved by competitors, ranked by estimated audience appetite and your ability to execute given your resources.

## Scope

- Designed for: ecommerce operators, TikTok Shop sellers, brand teams, content strategists
- Platform context: TikTok Shop, Instagram, YouTube, platform-agnostic social commerce
- Language: English

## Limitations

- This skill analyzes content you provide manually — it does not crawl live competitor accounts or pull real-time data from any platform API.
- Engagement rate estimates are directional and based on content pattern inference, not actual platform analytics; treat them as relative signals rather than absolute benchmarks.
- Recommendations assume your brand has reasonable flexibility in content scheduling; brands with rigid editorial calendars or compliance review requirements may need to adapt the counter-programming windows suggested.
